### Cultural Startup

Master's research project exploring folklore ornaments, participatory design, and meaning-making in cultural identity.

The project draws on **folklore as a collective authorship model**. Historically, national patterns emerged through distributed craft practices — distilling shared traits into ornament. This research proposes a contemporary analogue: a digitally mediated folklore process.

Meaning is treated as dynamic and context-dependent. Authority over symbolic definition shifts from institutions to participants. The ornament becomes a living interface between individual perception and collective identity.

### Question

Who defines the meaning of an ornament?
* What are the characteristics of the Belarusian national ornament?
* How can abstract ornamental motifs be described and interpreted?
* What can an act of reading ornament reveal about the people of Belarus?
* How to balance inclusion with the material constraints in national identity?
* How can the process of folklore authorship be replicated through digital technology?
- Is the final output a democratic average or a map of plural identities?

### Goal

Explore how a crowdsourced, generative process can produce new ornamental variations reflecting diverse, present-day Belarusian perspectives — while maintaining cultural recognisability.
